Who are we

Student Initiated
Oral Record Collection Project

Initiated by seven students from Tsinglan high school, IDOH is an oral “herstory” collection project interviewing female migrant workers in the city of Dongguan, Guangdong. We aim to record the life stories of female migrant workers from three dimensions: personal life, work experience, and societal perspective, and present the information through exhibition, WeChat official account, transcripts, and website for public access.

Traditional and mainstream historiography has always focused on historical events, treating people as appendages while ignoring the fact that people are actually the main subject of history. Moreover, traditional history has mostly depicted the stories of great men, while the voices of the ordinary people, who are the major participants of historical process, had been lost. IDOH chooses interviewees, especially women, regardless of fame and status, as we hope to make up for the lack of female voices in a male-dominated history.

Members of IDOH all live in Dongguan, a city known as the "factory of the world". Since the reform and opening up, Dongguan, as a coastal region, has attracted a large number of overseas investors to build factories here. Although we have witnessed the economic development of Dongguan, we seldom acknowledge the contribution of a large group of migrant workers behind the scene , in which most of them are women.(as most of the work is meticulous manual work, enterprises generally prefer women when recruiting workers)

Therefore, through the IDOH project, we hope to dive into the roots of Dongguan and approach the life of those who support this land silently. The memories of the female workers will serve as a unique and valuable information resource to promote the society’s understanding of the fate of individuals in the flood of modern industrialization, so as to form a deeper, more inclusive bond among people. At the same time, we hope that the interview process will become an opportunity for the interviewees to reflect their own life experiences, listen to and explore their inner voices.

WHAT IS ORAL HISTORY

Something About Oral History

In simple words, oral history is to record the original conversations of the interviewees with researchers, using modern technology, such as transcripts, audio recordings, and video recordings, and to compile the information into a written script after verifying them with literature sources. As an important source of information about society and history, oral history happens every day in very natural forms: the rambling reminiscences of the past from the elderly to the youngest in the family, the passing down of skills from a craftsman to an apprentice... Each of us can do oral history or be the subject of oral history, it’s just that most of the time, we do not realize the significance of recording and disseminating oral history. Oral histories are not only historical references for future generations, but also an opportunity for individuals to reflect on their life experiences, listen to their inner voices, and carry the emotions of their relatives, which can foster a sense of unity within a community.
